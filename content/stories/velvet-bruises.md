# Velvet Bruises

*You don't have to be fine. You just have to be honest about not being fine.*

Rui had been performing being okay for so long that he sometimes forgot he was performing. He was sixteen and his father had lost his job fourteen months ago and the house had a new quietness in it, not peaceful but pressurised, and Rui had decided early on that the correct response was to give no one any additional problems. He was good at school. He helped with dinner. He said things were fine when people asked. He had become expert at the particular facial expression of a person who has nothing to report.

His friend Catarina was the one who noticed. Not through any dramatic revelation — she simply said, one afternoon in the park, "You don't have to do that," and did not specify what that was, because she didn't have to. Rui said, "Do what?" Catarina said, "Whatever that is that you're doing." He looked at the ground for a while. He thought about denying it. He thought about the fourteen months of not denying it to anyone, the accumulation of fine, the weight of a word said consistently until it meant nothing.

He told her. Not all of it — some things had been private for so long they'd become structural, part of how he held himself up — but enough. The job, the house, the way the quietness had sound to it if you listened, the performance of being fine in rooms where everyone else was also performing being fine. She listened without saying much. She didn't fix it. She just received it, which was all he had actually needed.

After that he was not immediately better, because honesty doesn't work like that. But he was different — fractionally, importantly different. The gap between what was true and what he was saying had closed slightly, which meant he was using less of himself to maintain the distance. He still said fine sometimes. But now, occasionally, he said something else instead, and the bruise showed, and nobody left, and the sky did not fall in, and slowly he began to understand that vulnerability was not what broke connection — it was what made it.

---

*The bruise you hide doesn't disappear. It just means you carry it alone. You don't have to carry it alone.*
