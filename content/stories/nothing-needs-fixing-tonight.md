# Tuesday

*Not every evening is a project.*

Nothing happened on Tuesday evening.

That is the whole story. Nothing happened. Elena came home from work at six-fifteen, took off her shoes in the hall, changed into the loose trousers that did not need to be anything, and made tea. She did not meditate. She did not journal. She did not do the workout she'd planned. She did not respond to the three messages she'd been meaning to respond to.

She sat on the sofa with her tea and watched the evening light change on the wall opposite the window.

The light started gold and went slowly orange and then something darker and then the room was dim and she didn't turn the lamp on for a while because the dim was fine.

She thought about things, vaguely. Nothing organised. Her mind moved around at its own pace without a destination, the way it does when you're not trying to direct it anywhere. She remembered something from her childhood that she hadn't thought about in years, for no particular reason.

At some point she turned the lamp on. At some point she ate something. At some point she went to bed.

In the morning she felt — not transformed, not restored, not like a better version of herself. Just fine. Just rested. Just like a person who had given themselves an evening that didn't require anything.

She hadn't fixed anything. Nothing had needed fixing.

That had been enough.

---

*Some evenings the only task is to be in them.*
