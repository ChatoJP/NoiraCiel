"I Never Knew any other way."
Chapter 11 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: The tenderness of simplicity — a life lived without alternatives, full of its own kind of grace.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. coastal village, dawn rituals, faded warmth, familiar streets, simple grace. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "I grew up watching quiet people doing what they said they'd do / Never needing recognition, never chasing someone else'." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
