"Always In Your Corner."
Chapter 09 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: The phone call that changes the quality of darkness — someone always present.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. rain on window, single lamp, phone call, morning light, thin curtains. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "There'll be nights when the road disappears And every mile feels longer than the years / When your own reflection turns." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
