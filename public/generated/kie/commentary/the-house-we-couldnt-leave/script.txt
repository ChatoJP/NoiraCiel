"The House We Couldn't Leave."
Chapter 10 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: The family home as a living thing — how spaces hold the memory of those who loved them.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. family home, glowing windows, dark field, left-behind objects, closing door. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "You found me staring at the ceiling again Three in the morning, talking to ghosts in / My head The television flickered." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
