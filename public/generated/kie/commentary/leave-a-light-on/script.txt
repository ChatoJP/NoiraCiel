"Leave A Light On."
Chapter 12 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: The lit window as love's most silent language — a mother's vigil made visible.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. lit window, dark exterior, silhouette, waiting, warm light on cold stone. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "I know you'll have your own roads, your own storms to outrun / You'll chase a thousand sunsets before your day is done." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
