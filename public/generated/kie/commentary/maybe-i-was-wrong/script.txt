"Maybe I was wrong."
Chapter 15 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: The courage of revision — the grace of returning to say what you should have said.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. kitchen table, morning coffee, same room years later, changed light, an opening. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "I spent years collecting answers Like they were trophies on a shelf / Always trying to be the smartest guy In every roo." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
