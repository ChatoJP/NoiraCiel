"Free Men Tell the truth."
Chapter 17 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: Freedom as clarity — the liberation that comes from speaking truthfully, whatever the cost.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. public square, speaking, listening, changed atmosphere, conviction, open sky. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "Oh, there was a boy in a small town who learned to keep his head down / He learned some doors stayed open if you never." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
