"The Empty Chair."
Chapter 13 of The Life Lessons I Hope You Learn.


This song came from a single question I couldn't stop asking myself: Grief that has found its proper place — the presence of the absent, held with dignity.

I wasn't looking for a clean answer. I was looking for the right way to carry the question.

When I wrote this, I kept coming back to certain images. empty chair, Sunday table, afternoon light, place setting, sacred absence. Not because they're poetic — because they're honest. These are the images that carry weight without trying to.

The lyrics started as a fragment: "There's a chair beside the window where the morning sunlight falls / Nobody has moved it lately, nobody at all / Somet." Everything else grew from that place.

If this song reaches you at the right moment — I hope it gives you permission.
Permission to ask. To sit with not knowing. To feel the weight without rushing to put it down.

That's all any of this is for.
